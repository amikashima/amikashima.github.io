Building a Data Analysis Tool for Sport Stacking Athletes

Why I built this tool

As a member of Japanâ€™s national team in sport stacking, I wanted a better way to track and understand my performance without the old routine of jotting down times on paper or my phone and manually calculating some analytics. This motivated me to create a multilingual web app called StackAnalysis, designed to help sport stacking athletes understand their speed and consistency.

From Paper to Web

I taught myself HTML, JavaScript, and CSS using W3Schools and built a simple tool that calculates basic performance metrics (averages, range, standard deviations, clean attempt rate) and generates visualisations from user-inputted times.

The website is available in three languages: English, Japanese, and Simplified Chinese. I embedded the script on Wix to make it publicly available, attracting users from 22 countries, including national team athletes from Singapore, Japan, China, and the US.

Reflection

Since this was my first frontend web development project, and one completed before AI chatbots were widely available, solving problems independently and seeing the webpage behave precisely as I intended was immensely rewarding.

Reflecting on this project three years later, there are additional features I would now love to add, including user accounts to save training history and more effective visualisations (e.g. histograms and time-series plots to track performance trends).

Still, as my very first programming project, StackAnalysis holds a special place for me that sparked my love for solving problems through code!
